Your Ingredient Guide

Understanding what goes onto your skin is the first step to a healthy glow. Here are the top ingredients we recommend based on your skin type:

Glycerin

Best for: All skin types

Versatile/gentle humectant suitable for all skin types, particularly those with dry, sensitive, or irritated skin needing deep hydration and barrier repair

Acts as a moisturizer that pulls water into the skin, making it excellent for combating dry, and flaky skin

Mandelic Acid

Best for: Sensitive, Acne-prone, or Oily skin

Individuals with sensitive, acne-prone, or oily skin, particularly those with deeper skin tones, seeking to treat acne, hyperpigmentation, and uneven texture.

Its large molecule size and slow absorption, it is far less irritating than other AHAs, excellent for sensitive skin types, rosacea, and anti-aging.

Niacinamide

Best for: Oily & Acne-Prone Skin

Helps visibly minimize enlarged pores, tighten lax pores, and improve uneven skin tone.

Hyaluronic Acid

Best for: Dry & Dehydrated Skin

A powerful humectant that keeps skin plump, hydrated, and helps reduce the appearance of fine lines.

Ceramides

Best for: Sensitive Skin

Lipids that help form the skin's barrier and help skin retain moisture while protecting against environmental irritants.

Salicylic Acid (BHA)

Best for: Acne & Clogged Pores

An oil-soluble acid that penetrates deep into pores to dissolve debris and reduce breakouts.

Vitamin C

Best for: Dullness & Dark Spots

A powerful antioxidant that brightens skin tone and protects against environmental damage.

Retinol

Best for: Fine Lines & Texture

A Vitamin A derivative that speeds up cell turnover to reveal smoother, younger-looking skin.

Squalane

Best for: All Skin Types / Barrier Repair

A lightweight, non-greasy oil that mimics your skin's natural oils to lock in intense moisture.

Centella Asiatica (Cica)

Best for: Sensitive & Irritated Skin

A medicinal herb known for its incredible soothing properties and ability to calm redness.

Glycolic Acid (AHA)

Best for: Rough Texture & Aging

Exfoliates the surface of the skin to remove dead cells, leaving the complexion glowing and soft.

Azelaic Acid

Best for: Rosacea & Hyperpigmentation

Gently decreases inflammation and kills acne-causing bacteria while fading stubborn dark marks.

Ingredients to Avoid

Here are some ingredients we recommended avoiding based on your skin type!

Unrefined / Low-quality Mineral Oils

Avoid: Acne-prone, Oily, or Sensitive skin

Occlusive, can create a barrier on the skin trapping excess oil and bacteria, leading to possible clogged pores, breakouts and dermatitis

Cocoa/Shea Butter

Avoid: Acne-prone, oily, or combination skin

These skin types should avoid or limit use, as it has a high comedogenic rating (3-4) that may clog pores. Both butters may be too heavy or greasy

Silicones (like Dimethicone):

Avoid: Acne-prone, Oily, or highly Sensitive skin

While non-comedogenic, silicones create an occlusive barrier possibly trapping dirt, oil, and bacteria, leading to breakouts and congestion.

May also feel too heavy for oily skin or cause irritation in sensitive individuals.

Synthetic Fragrance

Avoid: Sensitive, allergic, eczema-prone, rosacea-prone, and dry skin

Avoid fragrance to prevent irritation, contact dermatitis, redness,or itching

Can be a primary trigger for skin reactions,offering no beneficial properties, safer alternatives include fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products.

SD Alcohol (Specially Denatured Alcohol)

Avoid: Dry, sensitive, eczema-prone, and rosacea-prone skin

Used in skincare to make products feel lightweight and matte. It strips natural oils, damages the skin barrier, and causes irritation

Citrus Oils

Avoid: Dry, Sensitive, eczema/rosacea-prone skin

Citrus oils, such as lime peel oil and lemon oil, can increase sensitivity to sunlight and cause irritation and allergies.
